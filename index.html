Between the industrial
and the handmade,
a thinking tool.

Contemporary garment craft has bifurcated. On one side, fully industrialised production — fast, cheap, de-skilled. On the other, couture handcraft — slow, precise, gatekept by decades of tacit knowledge. The middle ground, where a designer can think through a pattern, is vanishing. Fabri-Form is a prosthetic for that middle.

01 · On Intra-action

Following Barad (2007), the pattern is not drawn by the algorithm nor by the designer alone, but emerges between them. The sliders and the sketch are two hands reaching toward the same garment from different directions.

02 · On the Middle

Craft today collapses toward two poles — mass industry or artisan mastery. This tool argues for the designer-who-thinks-in-patterns, an agent who neither subcontracts construction nor performs it alone.

03 · On What Comes Next

Stage 02 will replace the screen with a pan-tilt laser that projects these lines onto cloth in real space. The flat pattern is provisional; the final site is fabric, scissors, and hand.
